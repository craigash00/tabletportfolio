Great Lakes

The Great Lakes are a series of freshwater lakes in the mid-west of America. These lakes contain 21% of the World's fresh water.
There are five lakes within the Great Lakes: Erie, Michigan, Superior, Huron, and Ontario.

The great lakes cover a large area of the mid-west. Because of this, there are many amazing things to see and do along the Great Lakes.

Niagra Falls

Niagra Falls is part of the Niagra River that drains into Lake Erie and Lake Ontario. It is located in Buffalo, New York and Toronto, Ontario. Niagra Falls has the highest flow rate of waterfalls in America. More than six million cubic feet of water flow over the falls every minute. The total height of Niagra Falls is 167 ft.

Mackinac Island

Mackinac Island is an island in the State of Michigan that covers 4.35 square miles. The island is a very popular summer tourist destination. Most of the buildings on the island have had historical preservation. The island itself is listed as a National Historical Landmark. It is located in Lake Huron. Mackinac Island has a population of only 470.

Chicago

Chicago is a major city located on Lake Michigan in Illinois. Chicago is the third highest populated city in the United States Chicago gets as many as 58 million tourists per year. Chicago is home to Millenium Park, the Art Institute of Chicago, and the Limcolm Park Zoo. With numerous restaurants, museums, and attractions it is definitley a city to see!

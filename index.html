Great Lakes

The Great Lakes are a series of freshwater lakes in the mid-west of America. These lakes contain 21% of the World's fresh water.
There are five lakes within the Great Lakes: Erie, Michigan, Superior, Huron, and Ontario.

Michigan

Lake Mighigan is the second largest out of all five Great Lakes. Lake Michigan is connected along the American states of Michigan, Wisconsin, Illinois, and Indiana. Lake Michigan is home to large ports in Chicago, Milwaukee, and Green Bay.

Michigan has a surface area of 22,404 square miles and a maximum depth of 923ft. It is the only Great Lake completely in the United States. It is the fifth largest lake in the world.

Superior

Lake Superior is the largest out of all five Great Lakes. Lake Superior is connected to the Canadian Province of Ontario along with the American states of Michigan, Wisconsin, and Minnesota. Lake Superior drains into Lake Huron.

Michigan has a surface area of 31,700 square miles and a maximum depth of 1,333ft. Lake Superior holds 10% of the World's freshwater. It is the largest lake in the world by surface area.

Huron

Lake Huron is the second largest out of all five Great Lakes. Lake Huron is connected to the Canadian Province of Ontario along with the American state of Michigan. Lake Huron drains into Lake Erie.

Huron has a surface area of 23,007 square miles and a maximum depth of 750ft. Lake Huron is seperated from Lake Michigan by the 5 mile wide straight of Mackinac.

Ontario

Lake Ontario is the smallest out of all five Great Lakes. Lake Ontario is connected to the Canadian Province of Ontario along with the American state of New York. Lake Huron drains into the Atlantic Ocean.

Ontario has a surface area of 7,340 square miles and a maximum depth of 802ft. Lake Ontario is the only Great Lake that does not border Michigan.

Erie

Lake Erie is the fourth largest out of all five Great Lakes. Lake Erie is connected to the Canadian province of Ontario, along with the American states of Michigan, Pennsylvania, Ohio, and New York.

Erie has a surface area of 9,990 square miles and a maximum depth of 210ft. It is the shallowest of the Great Lakes. Erie drains via the Niagra River and Niagra Falls.
